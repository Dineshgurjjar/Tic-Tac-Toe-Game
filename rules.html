Tic-Tac-Toe Rules

Objective

The objective of Tic-Tac-Toe is to be the first player to get three of your marks (X or O) in a row, column, or diagonal on the game board.

Game Setup

  1. Board: Tic-Tac-Toe is played on a 3x3 grid. Optionally, it can also be played on larger grids like 4x4 or 5x5.
  2. Players: The game is played between two players, typically represented by X and O.

Gameplay

  1. Turns:
    • Player X always goes first.
    • Players take turns marking empty cells on the board with their respective symbols (X or O).
  2. Winning Condition:
    • A player wins by getting three of their marks in a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal row.
    • If all 9 cells are filled and neither player has achieved a winning condition, the game is a draw.
  3. Game Flow:
    • Player X starts the game.
    • Players continue alternating turns until one player wins or the game ends in a draw.

Additional Notes

  • The game can be played with a paper and pencil, or digitally as in this case.
  • Strategies involve trying to create a winning line while blocking the opponent from doing so.
  • Tic-Tac-Toe is a simple yet strategic game that helps develop basic pattern recognition and strategic thinking skills.
